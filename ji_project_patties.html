Quest for the Perfect Pike Patty

Waters near my Summer cottage provide an overabundance of pike compared to other fish.
As an avid fisherman this has posed somewhat of a quandary for keeping my meals interesting, and I've aimed to fix that in various ways.

My most recent attempts have been Indian influenced pike patties, and in the third iteration I think I managed to perfect it.

The Recipe

  • 1 kg pike
  • 2 shallots
  • 1 single clove garlic
  • 50 grams of ginger
  • 1 medium hot chili
  • 2 tablespoons of garam masala
  • 2 tablespoons of Kashmir chili powder
  • 2 tablespoons of potato flour
  • 2 teaspoons of maple syrup
  • 0.5 dl of soy sauce
  • 2 dl cream
  • 2 tablespoons of sesame oil
  • few fistfuls of breadcrumbs
  1. Grind shallots, garlic, ginger, chili, and the dry spices together with the pike twice or thrice depending on your meat grinder.
  2. Add soy sauce slowly to your potato flour to avoid clots and then mix it with rest of the liquids.
  3. Mix the liquids with the ground fish.
  4. Add breadcrumbs until a patty holds its form and doesn't stick too badly to a baking paper.
  5. Fry on medium heat and add salt based on taste.
You can basically serve them with anything but I found them to be excellent in fishburgers paired with horseradish flavoured mayo.
